Proof by Contrapositive  The *contrapositive *of  𝑃 ⇒ 𝑄 is the implication  ¬𝑄 ⇒ ¬𝑃.  A proof by contrapositive of  𝑃 ⇒ 𝑄 is a direct proof of  ¬𝑄 ⇒ ¬𝑃 .   Another way of putting it: the contrapositive of “if  𝐴 , then  𝐵 ” is  “if not  𝐵 , then not  𝐴 .”  Worksheet examples: a) Let  𝑛 be an integer. Proof by contradiction  To show that  𝑃 ⇒ 𝑄 is true by contradiction we show that  ¬ 𝑃 ⇒ 𝑄 ⇒⊥ (a contradiction).  Since  ¬(𝑃 ⇒ 𝑄) is logically equivalent to  (𝑃 ∧ ¬ 𝑄) , we want to show  that  𝑃 ∧ ¬ 𝑄 ⇒⊥ (a contradiction).  Worksheet example: 2 is irrational 8 CSC 225: Algorithms and Data Structures I -- Anthony Estey
